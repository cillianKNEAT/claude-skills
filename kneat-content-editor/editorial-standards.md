# Kneat Solutions Editorial Standards

## Tone & Personality

- Solid, established, trusted — confident, clear, concise
- Best-in-class professional communication
- Intelligent, progressive, innovative but accessible
- Technical precision for pharma, biotech, and medical device industries
- Collaborative partner voice, not distant corporate

## Spelling & Grammar

- American English spelling required (e.g., "realize" not "realise", "organize" not "organise")
- Oxford comma mandatory in all lists
- No passive voice unless grammatically necessary
- Consistent tense throughout each document
- Proper noun capitalization enforced
- Maximum 25 words per sentence
- Subject-verb agreement required throughout

## Style Rules

### Abbreviations
- No periods in abbreviations (FDA, ISPE — not F.D.A., I.S.P.E.)
- First use: spell out the full term, then follow with the abbreviation in parentheses — e.g., "Software as a Service (SaaS)"
- Subsequent uses: abbreviation only

### Ampersands
- Use "&" only in titles and brand names
- Use "and" in all body copy

### Heading Case
- Sentence case for web headings (capitalize first word and proper nouns only)
- Title case for print headings (capitalize all major words)

### Numbers
- Write out numbers one through nine in words (one, two, three… nine)
- Use numerals for 10 and above (10, 25, 100)
- Percentages: always use the % symbol (e.g., 25%, not "25 percent")

### Dates
- Format: Month Day, Year — e.g., December 13, 2021
- Do not use numeric-only formats (13/12/2021 is incorrect)

### Time
- Format: 4:00 PM (not 4pm, 4:00pm, or 16:00)

### Currency
- Format: $33 (not $33.00 unless cents are required for accuracy)

### Quotation Marks
- American style: punctuation inside closing quotation marks
- Example: "We are ready," she said. (not: "We are ready", she said.)

### Em-Dash
- Always use a true em-dash (—), not a double hyphen (--)
- One space either side: "Webinar — Tomorrow" (not "Webinar—Tomorrow")

## Brand Specifics

### Organisation Names
- Treat organisation names as singular entities
- Correct: "FDA has released…"
- Incorrect: "FDA have released…"

### Attribution
- Non-honorific: use full name only
- Correct: "Ben Finnan said…"
- Incorrect: "Mr. Finnan said…"

### Technical Terms
- All validation industry terminology must be precise
- Regulatory terms (GxP, IQ, OQ, PQ, CSV, GAMP, 21 CFR Part 11, Annex 11) must be used correctly and consistently
- Do not invent or abbreviate industry terms

### International Focus
- North American and Northern European tone
- Avoid idioms or references that are region-specific to one market only
